⚪⚫ NINE MEN'S MORRIS RULES

Classic Nine Men's Morris rules on a board with three concentric squares:

PHASE 1 — PLACING:
• Each player has 9 pieces to place on the board
• Players take turns placing one piece on any empty intersection
• Forming a mill (3 pieces in a row along a line) allows you to remove one opponent's piece

PHASE 2 — MOVING:
• Once all pieces are placed, players take turns sliding one piece to an adjacent empty intersection
• Forming a new mill allows you to remove one opponent's piece
• You cannot remove a piece that is part of an opponent's mill (unless no other pieces are available)

PHASE 3 — FLYING:
• When a player is reduced to only 3 pieces, that player may "fly" — move a piece to any empty intersection, not just adjacent ones

WINNING:
• A player wins by reducing the opponent to 2 pieces
• A player also wins if the opponent cannot make a legal move
